WHAT CAUSES DRAIN CLOGGING?

  1. Improper Waste Disposal

    2. Improper waste disposal occurs when trash, chemicals, or other waste materials are not discarded correctly, leading to pollution, blocked drainage systems, and harm to environment.


  2. Accumulation of Sediment and Soil

    3. Fine particles of dirt, sand, silt, and clay are washed into drainage systems by stormwater runoff, particularly from construction sites, agricultural areas, and eroded lands.


  3. Poor Drainage Design and Maintenance

    4. Allowing debris, sediment, and roots to build up, blocking flow, while also creating improper slopes that prevent water for reaching drains.


  4. Overpopulation and Urbanization

    5. These issues are particularly acute when urban development is unplanned or poorly managed often seen in rapidly growing cities in developing countries.
